Trayambakeshwar Temple Rishikesh – Timings, Poojas, and History

Trayambakeshwar Temple Rishikesh is a Hindu temple in Rishikesh, Uttarakhand. It is one of the main holy shrines of Rishikesh. The temple is present near the famous Lakshman Jhula of Rishikesh. The temple is also one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. It is also a part of the Char Dham Yatra of Uttarakhand.

The temple is a 13-storeyed high building. Hence, it is also popularly called as ‘Terah Manzil Temple‘. The temple is having various idols of Hindu Gods and Goddesses. However, the shrine is mainly dedicated to Lord Shiva. The name ‘Trayambakeshwar’ meaning ‘the House of the three-eyed‘ suggesting it as the abode of Lord Shiva.

History of Trayambakeshwar Temple

Located near Lakshman Jhula, Trayambakeshwar Temple is one of the revered holy shrines in Rishikesh. It is also known as Terah Manzil Temple, which is a 13-storied building and houses the idols of numerous Hindu deities. The shrine is considered the abode of Lord Shiva, and it got a name after him only. Therefore, Trayambakeshwar means the same, ‘The Abode Of Three-Eyed’. The temple is believed to be one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Many sections in the temple comprise Vedic and religious works.

The temple gets flocked by a number of devotees and other travelers who come to visit Laxman Jhula- the hanging bridge. Trayambakeshwar Temple in Rishikesh is not only visited to seek blessings but also to enjoy the scenic surroundings formed by striking Himalayan ranges. Devotees also visit Swarg Niwas Mandir, which is in the vicinity of this sacred spot.

The temple remains open from 6 AM to 12 PM and 2 PM to 7 PM every day.

Once can witness the intricate designs and architectural brilliance on the walls and idols inside this 13-storied Trayambakeshwar temple. Considered to be more than 400 years old, it is one of the must-visit temples in Rishikesh. The temple is also one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. How to Reach Trayambakeshwar Temple

Rishikesh railway station is the nearest railhead from the temple, which is well-connected with the major cities and towns of the country by regular trains. The city is also well-linked with the other states by a good road network. From the bus stand and railway station, auto rickshaws are available to reach the temple. Also, you can hire an auto rickshaw for Lakshman Jhula from where you can walk till the temple..
